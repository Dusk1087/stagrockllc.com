About

Valuation is the foundation

Stag Rock was built around one question: does the price make sense? We apply that discipline across fixed income and equities, using the tools and frameworks appropriate to each.

The bond market is not like the stock market. It is fragmented, diverse, and often opaque. Many bonds don't trade on the open market. You're not just buying a company, you're buying into a credit stack where seniority of claims matters. Filtering that universe down to the bonds worth holding is the core of our fixed income process.

We use a quantitative model to work through the layers of that market systematically, identifying securities that meet the credit quality, maturity, and structural characteristics that make sense in a client portfolio. Credit risk assessment goes beyond the rating agencies, whose limitations were made clear in the global financial crisis. We supplement traditional ratings with Bloomberg's proprietary default probability model, which evaluates the financial characteristics of each issuer against a deep history of actual defaults to produce a more granular view of credit risk.

The goal is an individual bond ladder where, assuming no default, you know what you're getting from the day you buy it regardless of what happens with rates. You hold to maturity and collect what you were promised.

Our equity process combines two foundational valuation methodologies. The first is a relative value approach: a quantitative model that evaluates a broad universe of companies across fundamental characteristics, identifying names that appear statistically attractive or overbought compared to similar businesses. This is inherently a market-based lens, measuring how a company is priced against its peers.

The model's output is then balanced with diversified portfolio construction to ensure alignment with the broader economy. The goal is a portfolio that captures the signals the model identifies while maintaining the kind of sector and factor diversification that keeps a portfolio durable across market environments.

For a small number of names where the relative value signal is particularly compelling, we layer in an income-based approach: building a forward-looking view of the business, estimating future cash flows, and discounting them back to a present value. Combining both methods, what the market says a company is worth relative to its peers and what the cash flows say it is worth on its own terms, gives a more complete picture than either approach alone. Names that survive both lenses may become concentrated positions alongside the systematic core.

Fiduciary Standard

Research-Driven

Portfolios are built on proprietary models developed and maintained in-house, not outsourced strategies or third-party products.

The equity and fixed income models are applied across client accounts so that everyone tracks together and course corrections can be made systematically. Each household's allocation reflects its own circumstances, but the underlying research process is consistent.

Background

Reed Foster founded Stag Rock in 2018 after a career that spanned naval aviation, portfolio management, and investment banking. He spent eleven years as a naval officer and F/A-18 pilot, completing multiple deployments overseas and graduating from the Navy's Fighter Weapons School (TOPGUN) before serving as an instructor pilot. After the Navy, he worked as a portfolio manager and later as an investment banker at Moelis & Company in New York City, giving him experience across both sides of the capital markets before setting out on his own.

Reed is a CFA Charterholder and licensed CPA in Virginia. He holds an MBA from the College of William & Mary, a Master of Accounting from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, and a B.S. in Systems Engineering from the United States Naval Academy.

He continues to fly professionally as a Boeing 777 First Officer for a major cargo carrier. Flying globally as a cargo pilot provides a firsthand view of how goods, capital, and commerce move around the world, a perspective that informs how he thinks about markets.

Reed also operates Stag Rock CPA, PLLC, which allows him to take on private business valuation work on a case-by-case basis. These engagements draw on income-based and market-based approaches alongside precedent transaction analysis where control premiums and M&A context are relevant, and typically involve shareholder transactions, partner buyouts, or corporate planning.
